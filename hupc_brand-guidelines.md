## Typography (MANDATORY — follow exactly)

### Primary Typeface
**System UI / San Francisco**

| Weight | Value | Usage |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Light | 300 | Hero headlines, large display text |
| Regular | 400 | Body text, standard content |
| Medium | 500 | Subheadings, emphasis |
| Semibold | 600 | Section headers, buttons, labels |

### Type Scale & Hierarchy
| Level | Size | Weight | Line Height | Usage |
|-------|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| **H1** | 48px | Light (300) | 1.2 | Page hero titles |
| **H2** | 36px | Regular (400) | 1.3 | Section headings |
| **H3** | 24px | Medium (500) | 1.4 | Subsection headings |
| **H4** | 20px | Medium (500) | 1.5 | Card titles, smaller headings |
| **Body Large** | 18px | Regular (400) | 1.6 | Featured body text |
| **Body** | 16px | Regular (400) | 1.6 | Standard body text |

### Typography Best Practices
| DO | AVOID |
|----|-------|
| Use light/medium weights for calm aesthetic | All caps for extended text |
| Maintain 1.5–1.6 line spacing for body text | Heavy weights for large blocks |
| Keep line length 50–75 characters | Spacing (line-height) less than 1.4 |
| Use Primary Blue for headlines | |

### Typography Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA)
- Minimum **16px** for body text
- **4.5:1** contrast ratio for normal text
- **3:1** for large text (18px+ or 14px+ bold)

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice Attributes
| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Empathetic** | We understand and validate feelings, showing genuine care |
| **Reassuring** | We provide comfort and confidence in our care |
| **Clear** | We communicate simply, avoiding jargon |
| **Culturally Responsive & Inclusive** | Language is never judgmental or exclusionary; honor each patient's background and lived experience |
| **Collaborative & Human** | We talk with patients, not at them; we speak like a trusted partner, not an institution |

### We Are / We Are Not
| We ARE | We Are NOT |
|--------|-----------|
| Warm and personal: "We're here for you" | Clinical or cold — avoid overly technical language |
| Respectful: "Your wellbeing matters" | Patronizing — don't talk down to patients |
| Hopeful: "Together, we'll find a path forward" | Overly casual — maintain professionalism |
| Professional: "Evidence-based treatment" | Stigmatizing — never shame or judge |
| Inclusive: "Everyone deserves quality care" | Vague — be specific about services |
| Action-oriented: "Take the first step today" | Pushy — respect patient autonomy |

### Key Messaging Pillars
| Pillar | Message |
|--------|---------|
| **Compassionate Care** | We treat the whole person with dignity, respect, and understanding |
| **Clinical Excellence** | Our team provides evidence-based, expert psychiatric treatment |
| **Hope & Healing** | Recovery is possible. We're committed to your journey to wellness |

### Messaging Do's & Don'ts

**DO:**
- Lead with something relatable and person-centered
- Use warm, inviting, and non-stigmatizing language
- Affirm dignity, respect, and cultural sensitivity
- Highlight accessibility and follow-through
- Be concise, reassuring, and solution-oriented
- Use positive, empowering language
- Reflect clinical expertise without feeling cold

**DON'T:**
- Sound overly clinical or detached (SAY: "We'll get back to you within 48 hours" NOT "Your request will be processed in 7–14 business days")
- Use impersonal or institutional wording (SAY: "You deserve care that really listens" NOT "Patients receive comprehensive evaluations at our multi-site locations")
- Minimize or dismiss emotional experiences (SAY: "What you're going through is real" NOT "Many people deal with this, so you're not alone")
- Use jargon without explanation (SAY: "We offer therapy approaches like CBT, proven to help" NOT "We offer CBT, DBT, ACT")
- Promise unrealistic timelines or outcomes (SAY: "Together, we'll work toward sustainable well-being" NOT "Feel better in 3 sessions!")
- Use language that feels transactional (SAY: "When you're ready, we're here" NOT "Sign up now!")
- Reinforce stigma or outdated clinical labels (SAY: "Living with," "Experiencing," "Managing" NOT "Mentally ill," "Suffering from")

## Digital UI Standards

### Website Visual Design
- Use `harmony-blue` (#2D5F8D) for headers and CTAs
- White backgrounds with subtle gray accents
- Generous padding (min 80px sections)
- Rounded corners (8–16px) for cards
- Soft shadows for depth (subtle elevation)

### Website Content Strategy
- Clear, concise copy with headings
- Patient-first language throughout
- Accessible forms with validation
- HIPAA-compliant security messaging
- Easy navigation with breadcrumbs

### Accessibility Requirements
| Standard | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| WCAG 2.1 AA | All digital properties minimum |
| Screen readers | Proper ARIA labels and semantic HTML |
| Keyboard nav | All interactive elements accessible via keyboard |

### UI Interaction Standards
- Transitions: **0.2–0.3s ease-in-out** for calmness
- Security: Clear visual trust indicators throughout

> "Digital touchpoints are often the first step in a patient's journey. We must prioritize trust and immediate reassurance above all else."

## Social Media Engagement Protocol

> "Engagement is about listening as much as speaking. In a clinical context, privacy and safety are the absolute priorities of every digital interaction."

### STRICT HIPAA MANDATE
Patient status should **never** be acknowledged publicly. All care-related inquiries must be redirected to secure, private channels.

### Response Protocol
| Action | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Acknowledge | Human-centered response |
| Neutral Voice | Remain clear and professional |
| Crisis First | Provide resources (988 or 911) |
| Privacy Guard | Redirect to private channels |

### Standard Responses
- **General feedback:** "Thank you for reaching out, we're happy to help."
- **Appointment query:** "I can help with that. Let me pull up your appointment details." (redirect to private)
